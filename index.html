  • Milestone 1-A Game of Checkers

    • Arthur Samuel was one of the first people to make a breakthrough in Artifical Intelligence. He managed to create a script to play checkers.In 1949 Samuel began his journey with his team. Infact once he began, he wasn't even writing a program for a machine that existed yet. His writing was on the IBM 701, also known as the defense calculator while in development. It was the first large non-numerical program, so it would dictate all subsequent IMB machines. Samuel wrote the checkers program as a set of small self-contained modules loaded by a central module. In the end of it all, his program reached an advanced amateur playing field for checkers. It was impressive for the time seeing as one man, Claude Shannon, wanted to make a program for Chess, which he failed at. Samuel completed his first checker program on the IBM 701, and when it was about to be demonstrated, Thomas J. Watson, Sr., the founder and president of IBM, said that the demonstration of the program would raise the price of IBM stock by 15 points. Remarkably, it did. Samuel was eventually put in charge of a pure research effort after persuading the Watsons that IBM needed to do pure research and not just product development.

  • Milestone 2-Unimation

    • In 1961 the very first industrialized robot is made. Named Unimate, it was created by the American inventor George Devol. The 4000 pound arm moved di castings from the assembly line and welded the parts onto car frames. This activity was incredibly dangerous for the workers of the establishment, seeing as the exhaust was poisonous, and the chance of losing a limb was certainly there. The very first prototypes for Unimate were controlled by vacuum tubes which were used as digital switches, although later versions used transistors. The “off-the-shelf” parts that were available in the 50’s were not adequate for the Unimates purpose and as a result the Unimate team had to create all the parts for the arm themselves. They invented many new technologies, specifically a rotating drum memory system that had data parity controls. In 1960, Devol sold the very first Unimate robot. It was shipped in 1961 from Danbury, Connecticut to General Motors. It was truly a turning point in industry. In 1966 full scale production began in Connecticut, where it was first shipped from, after several years of market surveys and rigorous field tests. Unimations first production robot was mainly a materials handling robot, where it mainly carried things around, but it was soon followed by a flurry of different types of robots such as ones for welding. Unimation began its first profits in the year 1975, 9 years after its first shipment out.

  • Milestone 3-The 90's Boom

    • One of the greatest booms in technology for AI would be in the 90’s. There were dozens of major advances in every single area of AI technology. Significant demonstrations included machine learning, where machines could improve significantly with repeated actions. Another is intelligent tutoring, where machines could now teach others. Case-based reasoning was even more interesting. Machines could now reason, it was unheard of. Data-mining was another breakthrough where machines could now manually data-mine information off of specific things chosen. One that we most likely use today is translation. Nobody wants to waft through a multilanguage dictionary, so machines now being able to translate others for us was one of the most world breaking advances yet. It broke the barrier for billions of people now that they could communicate significantly easier. Another is games. It seems normalized now, but back then games having systems of thought and choice was spectacular.

  • Milestone 5-Self-Driving Cars

    • Self-driving cars are the breakthrough of the era. Self-driving cars are one of the greatest inventions of the 21st century. For not only being spectacular for convenience but also safety with taking our human error from the equation of road-travel. Tests have been conducted all the way back to the 1920s in Japan, and trials officially began in the 50’s. A telltale of how much time and effort has gone into the technology. Yet even still we do not have complete autonomy. All the Self-driving cars are not fully self-driving. All of them would be classified as “Advanced Drivers Assistance System.” Remember that autonomous means to be self-governing. The current day “self-driving cars” would be seen as automatic. If we reach full autonomy with vehicles then it would be able to compensate for system failures without external intervention. Seeing as how this seems very difficult to accomplish, it may be another several decades till we reach full autonomy, but only time can tell. The types of technology are distinguishable from the other types of vehicles on the market. Autonomous vehicles are able to be more “transformative and agile to possible changes.”
